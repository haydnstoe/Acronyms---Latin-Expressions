Acronyms

Example Sentences

ADR – Alternative Dispute Resolution

“The parties agreed to attempt ADR before issuing proceedings in the High Court.”

CPR – Civil Procedure Rules

“Under the CPR, the claimant must file and serve a compliant witness statement by the specified deadline.”

ECHR – European Convention on Human Rights

“The applicant alleged that the decision breached Article 6 of the ECHR.”

ECJ – European Court of Justice

“The ECJ held that the national measure was incompatible with EU law.”

EAW – European Arrest Warrant

“The magistrates’ court considered whether the EAW was proportionate in the circumstances.”

IP – Intellectual Property

“The defendant was found to have infringed the claimant’s IP rights by distributing unauthorised copies.”

JR – Judicial Review

“The claimant sought JR of the local authority’s refusal to provide housing assistance.”

NDA – Non‑Disclosure Agreement

“The consultant signed an NDA prohibiting disclosure of confidential commercial information.”

T&Cs – Terms and Conditions

“The consumer argued that the T&Cs were unfair under the 2015 Act.”

UKSC – Supreme Court of the United Kingdom

“The UKSC dismissed the appeal, finding no error of law in the tribunal’s reasoning.”

Latin Expressions

Example Sentences

ab initio – from the beginning

“The contract was void ab initio due to fraudulent misrepresentation.”

bona fide – in good faith

“The purchaser acted bona fide and without notice of the prior charge.”

de facto – in fact / in practice

“He was the de facto manager despite lacking formal appointment.”

de jure – by law

“The council remained the de jure owner of the land.”

ex parte – by or for one party only

“The court granted an ex parte interim injunction pending a full hearing.”

habeas corpus – challenge to unlawful detention

“A writ of habeas corpus was sought to challenge the legality of the detention.”

ipso facto – by that very fact

“The breach did not ipso facto terminate the agreement.”

mens rea – guilty mind

“The prosecution failed to prove the requisite mens rea for the offence.”

prima facie – on the face of it

“The claimant established a prima facie case of discrimination.”

ultra vires – beyond legal powers

“The decision was quashed on the basis that the authority had acted ultra vires.”

  • ultra vires (correct)

  • de jure

Tier 3 – Production

Scenario: A court grants an urgent injunction without notifying the defendant. What type of application is this?
